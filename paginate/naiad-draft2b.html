A Quiet Puddle, A Strong Gust

The sound of the wind blowing through trees. Birds chirping in the branches. Sunlight gently warming my body. Grass tickling my arms.

I was lying in the grass in a forest clearing, a spring to my left flowing into a glasslike pond at my feet. By my best judgment, it was late morning. I leaned over and looked into the water. My reflection gazed back, a young woman with long wavy pale blue hair—almost like a blue version of blonde. I'd never seen this face before, but somehow I recognized it as my own.

The water seemed to beckon to me, like a mother calling her child into her embrace. Only intending to step in, I instinctively dove head-first into the pond. The entire world exploded with clarity. Before, my vision had been on the hazy side, a [familiar] nearsightedness. Now, the underwater space was in crisp focus. I almost seemed to feel everything around me as precisely as I could see it. Though the surface covered only around 1,000 square feet, it was surprisingly deep and felt delightfully spacious.

For the first time, I realized that my legs were coated in greenish-blueish scales, and my feet were somewhat webbed.

“Naiad.” The word slipped from my lips. That's what I was—something like a water spirit. With a flick of my feet, I darted forward. Yes, yes! Faster and faster I swam circuits around the pond. The water rushing across my entire body filled me with a deep energy and contentedness. Yes! This was life!

While I would have liked to swim forever in childlike bliss, I was getting tired and a little hungry. Food. Where was the food? What was the food? I could eat those fish staring at me with their judgmental little eyes, but then the tiny ecosystem in my pond might suffer.

So, I pushed my head out of the surface and looked around, hoping to find something before I truly got hungry. Though the wind had calmed, a gust-like sound came from above. I tilted my head just in time to see a large bird descending upon me.
